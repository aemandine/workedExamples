There are many ways to find the area of a triangle. Using Heron's Formula, one can find the area using A = sqrt(s*(s-a)*(s-b)*(s-b)), where s represents the semiperimeter of the triangle, or half the perimeter. If two sides (a, b) and the angle between them (C) are given, the area can be found using the formula 1/2 * a * b * sin(C). There are many ways to calculate information about a triangle, but sometimes it requires quite a bit of busywork. Why don't we write our own program to do all the work for us?

Write a program that allows the reader to choose between two options:

  • Starting with the length of all three sides
  • Starting with the length of two sides and the angle between them

Prompt the user for the information they have (either all three sides or two sides and an angle) and then calculate and output the following information:

  • Length of all sides
  • Perimeter
  • Area

Your output for option (1) should look like this:

  Welcome to the Triangle Calculator!
  What information do you have about your triangle so far?
  (1) Length of all three sides
  (2) Length of two sides and the angle between them
  Please choose 1 or 2: 1
  Length of side 1: 3
  Length of side 2: 4
  Length of side 3: 5
  ---
  Information about your triangle...
  Side 1: 3.0
  Side 2: 4.0
  Side 3: 5.0
  Perimeter: 12.0
  Area: 6.0
  ---

Your output for option (2) should look like this:

  Welcome to the Triangle Calculator!
  What information do you have about your triangle so far?
  (1) Length of all three sides
  (2) Length of two sides and the angle between them
  Please choose 1 or 2: 2
  Length of side 1: 5
  Length of side 2: 8
  Angle between them in degrees: 45
  ---
  Information about your triangle...
  Side 1: 5.0
  Side 2: 8.0
  Side 3: 5.694862378062897
  Perimeter: 18.694862378062897
  Area: 14.14213562373095
  ---

You'll need to add this line to the top of your file to access the math.sin(), math.cos(), and math.sqrt() functions, and the math.pi constant: import math

Note that math.sin() and math.cos() accept angles in terms of radians, so you'll need to convert from radians to degrees.

You can assume that the user will always type correct input. They will correctly type a 1 or a 2 to answer the first question, and they will not input the side lengths of an impossible triangle.
